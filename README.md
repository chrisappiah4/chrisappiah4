About Me:
My name is Christopher Appiah, and I am a senior Mechanical Engineering Major at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, graduating in May 2026. I am interested in control theory and its applications, and have experience exploring autonomous systems. In 2023, I worked in the Estimation and Controls Laboratory at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, where I analyzed the behavior of multiple pendulums incorporating design, modeling, and simulation. This involved using MATLAB and Simulink workspaces to compare and contrast error analysis across two simulation environments. Carrying over this knowledge in my UAV dynamics course, I will be completing multiple projects, such as: multicopter dynamics derivation and simulation, inner and outer loop controller design and implementation, and Kalman filter design for state estimation, using MATLAB, Simulink, and Gazebo. I am also familiar with electronic hardware configuration and systems. For a Machine Design Project, I was responsible for the Programmable Logic Controller for an automated sortation machine. Currently, for my capstone final project, I am implementing Arduino code that allows full wireless control of a morphing airfoil.
